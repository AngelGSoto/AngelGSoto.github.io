About Me

Welcome! I'm Luis Angel Gutiérrez Soto, an astrophysicist passionate about understanding the universe through observational data and machine learning. Born in Valledupar, Colombia, my academic journey has taken me across Latin America, enriching my perspective on science and collaboration.

Currently, I'm a postdoctoral researcher at the Instituto de Astrofísica de La Plata (CONICET-UNLP), where I focus on identifying planetary nebulae and symbiotic stars using multi-band photometric surveys and developing machine learning classification algorithms.

My research bridges traditional astronomical observation with modern data science techniques, working with large surveys like S-PLUS and J-PAS to uncover hidden celestial objects and understand stellar evolution processes.

Research Interests

Planetary Nebulae

Studying the final stages of stellar evolution through identification and analysis of planetary nebulae in large photometric surveys.

Machine Learning in Astronomy

Developing classification algorithms to identify emission-line objects and other celestial sources using multi-wavelength data.

Large Photometric Surveys

Working with S-PLUS, J-PAS, and other surveys to extract scientific insights from massive astronomical datasets.
